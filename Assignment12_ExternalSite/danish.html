About the pastry

Danish pastry is a flaky, layered, sweet pastry made from a yeast-leavened dough laminated with butter, similar to a croissant but richer with eggs and milk. Originating in Austria, it's called wienerbrød ("Viennese bread") in Denmark, where it's filled with jam, custard, or marzipan and topped with glaze, nuts, or sugar.
